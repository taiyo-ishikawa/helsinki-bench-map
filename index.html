Classification

City-registered — YLRE official registry (street & park). Includes YLRE+OSM matched pairs (5 m threshold).

Community-mapped — OSM benches absent from YLRE registry.

Nature / Park — YLRE park layer benches or OSM benches within park/forest polygons (YLRE Viheralue WFS).

Waterfront — Within 50 m of the Baltic Sea coastline (OSM coastline data).

Sidewalk — Benches within 10 m of an OSM footway, path, pedestrian way, or cycle path open to pedestrians (Overpass API, April 2026). Applied to both YLRE street-registered (YLRE_Katuosat_piste) and OSM-only benches; YLRE park benches (YLRE_Viherosat_piste) are always excluded. This proximity check ensures that plaza and square benches in the Katuosat layer are not misclassified as sidewalk benches. Covers 4,458 benches (35%).

Near Cafe — Benches within 100 m of an OSM cafe (amenity=cafe or amenity=coffee_shop). Cafe locations fetched via Overpass API (574 cafes, April 2026); distances computed in EPSG:3067 (metric) using a scipy cKDTree nearest-neighbour lookup. Covers 1,563 benches (12.3%).

Bench Model Groups

Derived from the ylre_material field in YLRE data. Only city-registered benches carry model information; OSM-only benches and records with blank or unrecognised values are excluded when any model group filter is active. Five groups are defined:

Wood & Steel (666 benches) — Helsinki's most widespread public bench type: a wooden seat paired with a durable steel or stainless-steel frame. Models: HKI-D1 (steel-leg street bench, incl. removable variant), D3 (standard accessible bench), D4 (basic single chair), D12 (general bench, wooden seat & steel frame), D13 (general chair, wooden seat & steel frame). Materials: dense pine (mänty) or oak (tammi) seat; hot-dip galvanised and powder-coated steel frame.

Metal (39 benches) — All-steel benches prioritising durability and minimal maintenance, typically placed at transit stops, squares, and high-footfall areas. Models: HKI-D9 (table-bench, predominantly steel), D10 (general bench, steel), D11 (general chair, steel).

Stone & Slab (122 benches) — Heavy, permanent installations using natural stone or concrete as the primary structural element. Models: HKI-D2 (bench with granite/natural-stone base), D16 (concrete seating base). Also includes HKI/HKR large and small slab benches and tree-surround benches. Materials: Finnish natural stone and pesubetoni (exposed-aggregate concrete).

Park & Heritage (263 benches) — Place-specific models designed for particular parks or historic landscapes. Models: HKI-D5 (simple forest bench for natural trails), D6 (Kaivopuisto traditional wooden bench), D7 (Esplanade Park bench with ornate cast-iron legs), D8 (birch-pattern-leg bench), D14 (historic area prefab model). Materials: cast iron (valurauta) and substantial timber with decorative finishing.

Named Brands (118 benches) — Off-the-shelf street furniture procured from external manufacturers rather than city-designed. Models include: Benkert, Victor Stanley, Sineu Graff (Siege Solo / Banquette City), Monena Wave, Lehtovuori Lahti (2100), and products from partners such as Extery and Metalco. Typically introduced in new development areas or commercially driven public-space projects.

Methodology

Data collected April 2026. Finnish → English translation. EPSG:3879 → WGS84 reprojection (pyproj).

Deduplication: 5 m threshold (scipy cKDTree). 3,040 merged YLRE+OSM pairs.

District stats: Areas in EPSG:3067. Population & age data aggregated from 84 Helsinki postal code areas (45/60 districts matched via spatial centroid join with Helsinki WFS postal code polygons).

Age data: Statistics Finland Paavo 2023 (paavo_pxt_12ey). % 65+ = sum of 65–69, 70–74, 75–79, 80–84, 85+ groups. % 0–17 = sum of 0–2, 3–6, 7–12, 13–15, 16–17 groups.
